# Renovation condition rubric — photos → scope tier

How to pick the renovation tier (`renovation_budget.py --tier`) from listing photos. The
assessment is done by reading the subject's listing photos (Read tool renders them) and
scoring condition against this rubric. **Always show the evidence and flag it for confirmation
— photos oversell, and condition is the swing risk on the whole deal.**

## What to look at (rank rooms by cost impact)

1. **Kitchen** — biggest single driver. Cabinet style/condition, countertop material
   (laminate vs stone), appliance age/finish, backsplash, layout.
2. **Bathrooms** — second driver, and there are usually 2+. Vanity, tile vs fiberglass/acrylic
   surround, fixture age, grout/caulk, toilet.
3. **Flooring** — type + wear: carpet (stains/age), sheet vinyl, scratched laminate, cracked
   tile, refinishable hardwood.
4. **Walls / paint** — scuffs, dated colors, wallpaper, holes, water stains.
5. **Mechanical clues** — old thermostat/furnace/water heater in photos, knob-and-tube hints,
   window-unit ACs (no central air).
6. **Exterior / curb** — roof age, siding/paint, driveway, landscaping, fascia/gutters.
7. **Red flags** — water stains on ceilings, cracks, mold, exposed subfloor, missing
   fixtures/appliances, "as-is" staging. Any of these pushes the tier UP regardless of the rest.

## Tier definitions (the $25 / $50 / $75 / $100 model)

| Tier | $/sqft | What the photos look like |
|---|---|---|
| **none** | $0 | Recently renovated / rent-ready. Modern updated kitchen (newer cabinets, stone counters, current appliances), updated baths, flooring in good shape, fresh neutral paint, no visible damage. Looks listing-staged-new. |
| **light** | $25 | Structurally fine, **dated but clean and functional**. Older-but-intact kitchen/baths, tired flooring (worn carpet/old vinyl), scuffed/dated paint, old light fixtures. Needs paint + flooring + fixtures + deep clean to reach STR-grade. No room or system work. |
| **moderate** | $50 | **Dated and needs real updating**, not just cosmetics. Kitchen and baths are original/worn and need remodeling (new counters/cabinets-or-reface, new vanities/surrounds), flooring throughout, likely aging HVAC/water heater. The typical well-kept-but-original older home. |
| **heavy** | $75 | **Worn / lived-hard / deferred maintenance.** Kitchen and baths need full remodel, visible damage, old systems due for replacement, possibly minor layout changes for STR appeal. "Needs work." |
| **full** | $100 | **Poor / original / distressed.** Everything outdated or damaged, signs of water/structural issues, gut-and-rebuild. "As-is / investor special / bring your contractor." |

## Calibrating the call

- **Score to the STR-ready bar, not the live-in-it bar.** A house someone could move into may
  still need a refresh to compete in the top quintile (the revenue tier StayAscend underwrites).
  A "livable but dated" home is usually **light**, not none.
- **Match reno to the revenue tier.** If photos say it needs a gut, the move-in comps are not
  your comps until the work is done — don't stack top-quintile revenue on a full-gut condition.
- **When photos are few, low-res, or obviously staged/old**, widen the estimate and lower
  confidence. Say so. Prefer the higher tier when genuinely unsure (condition surprises cost
  money, not save it).
- **Confidence levels:** HIGH = clear interior photos of kitchen + both baths + main living.
  MEDIUM = partial coverage. LOW = exterior-only, stale, or no photos — flag for a walkthrough.
- **Output:** state the tier, the $/sqft, the rehab $, the room-by-room evidence, a confidence
  level, and "confirm before relying on it."
